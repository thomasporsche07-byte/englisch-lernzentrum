✅ Aussagesatz
Subjekt+Verb (+s/es)
I → Verb
you → Verb
he → Verb + s/es
she → Verb + s/es
it → Verb + s/es
we → Verb
you (Pl.) → Verb
they → Verb
They play football every day.

2
❌ Verneinung
Subjekt+don't / doesn't+Verb
I → don't
you → don't
he → doesn't
she → doesn't
it → doesn't
we → don't
you (Pl.) → don't
they → don't
⚠️ Verb danach immer Grundform – kein -s!
They don't play football.
She doesn't play football.
3
❓ Ja/Nein-Frage
Do / Does+Subjekt+Verb?
Do / Does an den Anfang!
Verb ohne -s!
💬 Short Answer: Yes, … do/does. · No, … don't/doesn't.
Do they play football? – Yes, they do.
Does she play football? – No, she doesn't.
4
🔵 W-Frage
W-Wort+do/does+Subjekt+Verb?
Where · When · What · Who · How
– immer ganz vorne!
Where do you live?
What does he do at weekends?
5
⏱ Wann benutze ich es?
Gewohnheiten ·Fakten · Zeitpläne
Das Simple Present beschreibt:
• Gewohnheiten (etwas, das regelmäßig passiert)
• allgemeine Fakten und Zeitpläne
• Wahrheiten, die immer gelten
I go to school every day. (Gewohnheit)
The sun rises in the east. (Fakt)
The train leaves at 7 a.m. (Zeitplan)
6
📌 Signalwörter
always usually often sometimes never every day on Mondays
always–never: vor dem Hauptverb
every day, on Mondays: am Satzende
⚠️ Bei be: Signalwort steht nach be!
She always does her homework.
He plays football every day.
She is always late. ← be + Signalwort!
