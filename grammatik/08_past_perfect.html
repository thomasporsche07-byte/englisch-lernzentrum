Past Perfect

Die Vorvergangenheit – was schon vor einem anderen Ereignis in der Vergangenheit abgeschlossen war

⚡ Regeln auf einen Blick
1
✅ Aussagesatz
Subjekt+had+past participle
Alle Personen → had.
past participle (= 3. Verbform).
Kurzform: I'd / she'd / he'd / they'd
She had finished her homework.
They had gone home already.
2
❌ Verneinung
Subj.+hadn't / had not+past participle
hadn't = had not (Kurzform)
never steht zwischen had und past participle.
⚠️ past participle bleibt gleich – kein +s oder +ed!
He hadn't eaten breakfast.
I had never seen him before.
3
❓ Ja/Nein-Frage
Had+Subj.+past participle?
Had an den Anfang – immer!
Short Answers: Yes, … had. · No, … hadn't.
Had she finished her homework? Yes, she had.
Had he eaten? No, he hadn't.
4
🔵 W-Frage
W-Wort+had+Subj.+past participle?
Where · When · What · Why · How
– immer ganz vorne!
Where had she gone?
What had he done before dinner?
5
⏱ Wann benutze ich es?
Past Perfect →Simple Past
Das Past Perfect beschreibt, was zuerst passierte – noch vor einem anderen Ereignis im Simple Past.
⏳ PP passierte früher, SP passierte später.
When I arrived (SP), she had already left (PP).
← Sie ging zuerst weg, dann kam ich.
6
📌 Signalwörter
already just never before after when by the time
already / just / never: zwischen had und past participle
before / after / when: verbinden zwei Ereignisse
by the time: bis zu dem Zeitpunkt, als …
She had already left when I arrived.
By the time we got there, it had started.
