much · many · a lot of

Klasse 5 – Lighthouse 1, Unit 5 (LF 17, S. 155)

⚡ Regeln auf einen Blick
📚 Countable / Uncountable
1
Manche Nomen kann man zählen (countable), andere nicht (uncountable).
countable (haben einen Plural):
one apple → two apples
book, car, tomato, friend …

uncountable (kein Plural):
cheese, fruit, music, love
water, milk, bread, sugar …
✅ Bejahter Satz
2
In bejahten Sätzen nimmst du immer a lot of – egal ob countable oder uncountable.
Subjekt+Verb+a lot of+Nomen
We've got a lot of apples.
I eat a lot of fruit.
She has a lot of friends.
❌ Verneinter Satz
3
In Verneinungen unterscheidest du nach Nomen-Typ:
many bei countable · much bei uncountable
don't / doesn't+many/much
My dad doesn't watch many films. (films = countable)
I don't like much sugar in my tea. (sugar = uncountable)
❓ Fragen
4
Auch in Fragen: How many bei countable · How much bei uncountable.
How+many/much+Nomen
How many apples do we need?
How much money do you have?
💡 Eselsbrücke
5
Wenn du dir unsicher bist:
Lassen sich die Nomen zählen,
musst du immer many wählen.

Bei uncountable (Stoffe, Begriffe): much.
Im bejahten Satz immer am sichersten: a lot of.
⚠️ Häufige Fehler
6
❌ I have much friends. → ✅ I have many friends.
❌ How many bread? → ✅ How much bread?
❌ I drink much water every day. → ✅ I drink a lot of water every day.
(im bejahten Satz besser a lot of)
