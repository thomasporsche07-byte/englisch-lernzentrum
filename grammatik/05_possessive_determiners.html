1
✅ Wem gehört etwas?
Possessivbegleiter+Nomen
I → my (mein/e)
you → your (dein/e)
he → his (sein/e)
she → her (ihr/e)
it → its (sein/e/ihr/e)
we → our (unser/e)
you (Pl.) → your (euer/eure)
they → their (ihr/e)
I have a dog. My dog is brown.
She has a bike. Her bike is new.
They have a class. Their class is fun.
2
📐 Stellung im Satz
my/your/…+Nomen
Possessivbegleiter stehen direkt vor dem Nomen.
⚠️ Kein Artikel davor! Nicht „the my book“!
✅ This is my book.
❌ This is the my book.
✅ Her brother is nice. (ohne „a/the“)
3
⚠️ its vs. it's
its ≠ it's
its = sein/ihr (Possessivbegleiter, OHNE Apostroph)
it's = it is (Kurzform, MIT Apostroph)
💡 Trick: Kannst du „it is“ sagen? → it's. Sonst → its.
The cat plays with its ball. (= dem Ball der Katze)
It's a nice day. (= It is a nice day.)
4
⚠️ their / they're / there
their ≠ they're ≠ there
their = ihr/e (Possessivbegleiter)
they're = they are (Kurzform)
there = dort, da
💡 Klingen gleich – sind aber verschieden!
Their teacher is funny. (ihr Lehrer)
They're in the garden. (= They are …)
Look! Tom is over there. (dort)
