Passive Voice

Das Passiv – wenn das, was geschieht, wichtiger ist als die Person, die es tut

⚡ Regeln auf einen Blick
1
💡 Was ist Passiv?
Aktiv →Passiv
Aktiv: Wer macht etwas? Tom builds the house.
Passiv: Was passiert mit etwas? The house is built.
Wer es tut, ist im Passiv oft egal oder unbekannt.
Aktiv: Workers build cars.
Passiv: Cars are built.
→ Wichtig ist: was passiert (Autos werden gebaut).
2
📘 Simple Present Passive
Subjekt+am/is/are+past participle
Form von to be + past participle (3. Verbform).
past participle: regelmäßig + ed (worked, used) · unregelmäßig (built, written, spoken).
I am
you are
he is
she is
it is
we are
you are
they are
English is spoken in many countries.
The cars are made in Germany.
3
📙 Simple Past Passive
Subjekt+was/were+past participle
was/were + past participle.
was für Singular, were für Plural & you.
I was
you were
he was
she was
it was
we were
you were
they were
The phone was invented in 1876.
The pyramids were built long ago.
4
🔄 Aktiv → Passiv umformen
Subj. →Subj. (war Objekt) +be + PP
3 Schritte:
1. Objekt aus Aktiv → wird Subjekt im Passiv.
2. Verb-Form anpassen: Form von to be + past participle.
3. Altes Subjekt → optional mit by … oder weglassen.
Aktiv: Tom writes the letter.
Passiv: The letter is written (by Tom).
5
👤 by + Person
be + PP+by …
by nur, wenn die Person/Sache wichtig ist:
✅ Hamlet was written by Shakespeare.
❌ Weglassen, wenn klar/unbekannt: English is spoken … (überall).
The room is cleaned by my mum.
The window was broken. (Wer? – egal!)
6
❌❓ Verneinung & Frage
be + not /Be + Subj.?
Verneinung: Form von to be + not + past participle.
Frage: Form von to be kommt nach vorn.
The car isn't washed every week.
Were the keys found?
Where was it made?
7
⏱ Wann benutzt man Passiv?
unbekannt unwichtig Fakten Erfindungen Produktion
Passiv nutzt man, wenn …
• man nicht weiß, wer es tut
• die Person/Sache egal ist
• man Fakten oder Vorgänge beschreibt
English is spoken in many countries. (Fakt)
The phone was invented in 1876. (Erfindung)
My bike was stolen! (Wer? – unbekannt)
